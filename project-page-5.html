UX Game system Concept

Description

The original Sheep Tag is a game variant from the World of Warcraft game. Players would group up and play this game using the World of Warcraft's community and networking system.  

The goal was to assess whether there was a need for this game and if a stand alone option the best route.  

Responsibilities
  • User Research
  • Prototype
  • Journey Map
  • Competitive Audit
Duration

4 Weeks

Research

I started off with a Stakeholder interview. I got a lay-of-the land where to find the users and where they hang out. I did some secondary research reviewing Twitter, Reddit and Youtube videos.  I created 2 persona's one of the typical gamer and the other are the hardcore streamer based on the feedback from the social media accounts. 

I made a journey map of the users in the current game and kept track of how many steps it took to get into the game, how easy it was to understand how to play and how to get friends into your game. I made a list of paint-points that needed to be solved for. 

I did some competitive analysis.  Sheep Tag is not the only game variant that came from World of Warcraft. I looked at how these other games were dealing with their community, networking and user flows. 
I took what was working well visually from the other games. 

Analysis

From the research I did, I had enough information to come up with two types of gamers, the causal gamers who values games with friends for a laugh, and the hardcore streamer gamer who do it professionally. I collected the painpoints and gave them T-shirt sizing values. I made an effort-values metrics ranking them by the most value for the effort to come up with the MVP list for initial development. 

I created a sitemap and a wireframe for the different states and flow of the user through the game.  

Design

I was tasked to do the research and user interaction phase, so did a lo-fidelity mockup to demonstrate the structure of the game. 

Prototype

I created an interactive prototype to test internally with the game design team and the backers for the kickstarter for the Sheep Tag players. 
Prototype

Testing

The testing, we got a lot of feedback from the internal team and old school gamers of the original SheepTag. This feedback has helped shape the design of the game and the backers felt happy they got to contribute to a game they loved. 

Outcome

The prototype and other marketing helped fully fund the Kickerstarter of this game, and is scheduled to be released in 2022. The team has a better idea of what work is involved and the game system that needs to be created. All the heavy lifting is done and they can just create it. 

What I Learned

I came into this project while I was taking a UX Design course. If I were to do this project over, I would spend a lot more identifying and scheduling interviews with original sheep tag players. The social media content is good for a starting point but not good enough alone. Also, networking and community section was done at a high level. I feel I could have taken more time to research on that experience to help the developers know a little more how they are to solve that.
