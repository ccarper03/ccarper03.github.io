Web App Concept

Description

I was brought in as a consultant on top of my development responsibilities,  devoting 20% of my time . The goal was to have any vendor come with their studies and the sales team would display a branded web proposal and report securely online.  I was to assist in research, Information architecture and prototype the design of the web application they wanted to create and salesforce's development team would develop the web application using their platform. 

Role
  • Interactive Prototype
  • Visual Design
  • Analysis
  • Information Architecture
Date

6 months

Research

I started with qualitative and quantitative research. I scheduled time to do stakeholder interviews, then collected KPI's of the current process to keep track of where we started from. I gathered all the information that was expected to be displayed. 

Analysis

I created a single persona of a vendor, made a user flow and synthesized all the information down into  and organized information hierarchy. There were a couple of revisions of this, with every demo of the sitemap and wireframe sparked more ideas that needed to be added. 

Design

Once the Information Architecture was approved, we moved into visual graphics. I started off with a lo-fidelity mockup. Then filled in with content. 

Prototype

This was a rapid fire of revisions, not leaving much time for exploring. The primary concern was to get the MVP out to the Salesforce developers to see if this is something they can actually can do. 
Prototype

Testing

Testing with a vendor we worked closely with agreed to test the prototype. We got really good feedback from the user and would be excited to see that application go live. From the KPI stand point, being that the study would be accessible online to be viewable 24/7, already beat scheduling with people in different timezones to show the report. 

Outcome

This project was canceled due to this project exceeding it's budgeted timeframe.  The hand off to salesforce did not seem they could do what was promised, and extra developer man hours caused the team to stop.
